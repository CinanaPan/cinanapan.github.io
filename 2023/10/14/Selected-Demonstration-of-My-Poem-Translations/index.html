Selected Demonstration of My Poem Translations

Poem Translation

We Never Know

BY YUSEF KOMUNYAKAA

He danced with tall grass

for a moment, like he was swaying

with a woman. Our gun barrels

glowed white-hot.

When I got to him,

a blue halo

of flies had already claimed him.

I pulled the crumbled photograph

from his fingers.

There’s no other way

to say this: I fell in love.

The morning cleared again,

except for a distant mortar

& somewhere choppers taking off.

I slid the wallet into his pocket

& turned him over, so he wouldn’t be

kissing the ground.

我们永远不知道

尤瑟夫·科穆纳卡

他和高高的草一起跳舞

有那么一会儿，

就像他在和一个女人摇摆。

我们的枪管

发出白热的光。

当我走到他身边，

一圈蓝色的苍蝇

已经占领了他。

我从他的手指里

拽出一张皱巴巴的照片。

没有别的办法说这件事：

我其实早爱上了他。

早晨又清明了，

只有远处的迫击炮

和某处起飞的直升机。

我把钱包塞进他的口袋

并把他翻过来，

这样他就不会亲吻着地面。

Prose Translation

After the Fire

GREGORY FRASER

I heard you were going to Italy, he said. You heard correct, she said.

So you finally did it, he said, I’m happy for you. I’m happy for myself, she said.

One of Scarlatti’s sonatas poured through the wide French doors and a toast went up to the hosts, who announced they had been summoned by the spirits” to throw the “little soirée.”

Sometimes, he said, I feel so, I don’t know-droopy when I think of us, like a glove, he said, on a hand without fingers. Funny, she said, I sometimes feel like the hand. Wind moved like memory through a stand of pines, and then, as though a great umbrella sprang open, it was night. He looked absently off the veranda

and thought of days that followed her exit, stretched on end, uniform and blank, like pavement slabs. Do you miss me? he said.

Do you miss me? she said. I miss the self I was with you, he said.

On her face was written a letter torn and taped together.

She stared at the moon and saw it for what it was: one broken half of a bone china plate. Do you remember, she said, when you told me poetry is best written and best read by those who walk in their sleep?

He smiled. Do you remember, he said, when you told me a self-portrait is not a canvas you paint of yourself but one you paint yourself out of?

She let slip a trickle of laughter then shut the tap.

It was getting late. Soon the teacups would shine like small white lamps.

Soon the guests would find and vanish into their coats.

The way it kept expanding, he said, I was afraid my heart would blast apart. She trembled like the wire inside a light bulb but it didn’t show. This life is just the clang of a bell, he said. And death

its eternal echo, she said, finishing the thought as though they’d rehearsed.

They kept quiet then for what seemed like a very long while.

Venice? he asked, at last. Florence? Rome? None of these, she said.

I’m off to a tiny borgo in the Umbrian hills called Postignano.

He squinted with consideration. After the fire, he said. So the brochure said, she said.

After the Fire炉火之后

GREGORY FRASER

他说，听说你要去意大利了。

她说，你听得没错。

他说，你终于做到了，我为你高兴。

她说，我也为自己高兴。

一首斯卡拉蒂的奏鸣曲从宽敞的法式门里流淌出来，人们为主人们举杯祝贺，他们说是被“灵魂”召唤来举办这个“小派对”。

他说，有时候，我想到我们，我就觉得很，我不知道——萎靡不振，就像一只手套，在没有手指的手上。

她说，有趣，我有时候觉得自己就是那只手。

风像记忆一样穿过一片松树林，然后，就像一把大伞突然张开一样，天黑了。

他漫不经心地望着阳台外面，想起了她离开后的日子，一连串的日子，毫无变化和意义，就像一块块的水泥板。

你想我吗？他问。

你想我吗？她问。

我想念和你在一起的自己，他先开口。

她的脸上写着一封被撕碎又粘起来的信。

她盯着月亮，看清了它的本质：一个破碎的半边骨瓷盘子。

你还记得吗？你告诉过我诗歌最适合由那些梦游者阅读和创造？

他微笑了。那你还记得吗？你告诉过我自画像不是你画出自己的画布，而是你从画布上抹去自己？

她轻轻地笑了一声，然后关上了水龙头。时间已经不早了。不久茶杯就会闪耀着小小的白色灯光。不久客人们就会消失在他们的外套里。

心中的空洞不断地扩张，他说，我害怕我的心哪一天就会爆裂开来。

她像灯泡里的电丝一样颤抖，但没有表现出来。

这辈子就是一声钟响，他开口道。

而死亡是它永恒的回音，她接上。她接上他的话语仿佛事先排练过一样。

然后他们沉默了好像很长一段时间。

威尼斯？他问。佛罗伦萨？罗马？

都不是，她说，我要去一个叫做波斯蒂尼亚诺的乌姆布里亚山区小镇。

他眯起眼睛思考。火炉灭了后就离开吗？他问道。正如宣传册所说的那样，她答道。
